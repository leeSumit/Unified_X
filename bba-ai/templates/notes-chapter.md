### [Opening narrative heading — usually the historical event or company story title]

### [Conceptual explanation heading — the framework/concept name]

[400–600 words. Explain the concept in teacher-voice. Apply the Double-Explanation Rule: every concept explained twice — first with an everyday analogy (chai stall, cricket match, Swiggy delivery), then with a named Indian company using it today.

Structure:
- What it is (one sentence, plain language)
- The everyday analogy
- How it maps to the concept
- The business application (named company, specific outcome)]

### [Deeper mechanics heading — "The details that matter" or "[concept] in depth"]

[300–500 words. Cover nuances, edge cases, and common mistakes. Be honest about limitations — no framework works perfectly every time. Answer: "What do people get wrong?" and "What does mastery look like?"]

### [Business application heading — "[Company]'s approach to [topic]"]

[400–600 words. One or two specific companies using this concept today. Include:

- What went wrong first, then what they fixed
- A real quote from a real person (in italics with attribution)]
